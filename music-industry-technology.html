Music Business Model Evolution

Traditional Models

  • Physical Sales: CDs, vinyl, and physical distribution
  • Digital Downloads: iTunes, Amazon Music store
  • Radio Airplay: Broadcast and terrestrial radio
  • Live Performances: Concerts, tours, and venues
  • Licensing & Sync: TV, film, and commercial placements
  • Publishing: Songwriting and composition royalties

Emerging Models

  • Streaming Subscriptions: Monthly unlimited access models
  • Direct Fan Funding: Patreon, Bandcamp, and fan platforms
  • Music NFTs: Digital collectibles and ownership tokens
  • Virtual Performances: Online concerts and metaverse shows
  • Creator Economy: Social media and short-form content
  • Interactive Experiences: Gaming, apps, and immersive media

Revenue Distribution

Streaming accounts for 84% of music industry revenue, with subscription services generating $13.4B annually. However, artists receive only $0.003-$0.005 per stream, leading to exploration of alternative monetization models like NFTs, direct fan support, and blockchain-based platforms.
