Gaming Gear Guide

Keyboards, mice, headsets, monitors, and full PC builds — tested, ranked, and opinionated. No sponsored picks, no affiliate garbage, just what actually performs.

Gaming gear marketing is 90% nonsense. RGB lighting doesn't make you frag faster. "Gaming" branded anything costs 30% more than an identical product without the word "gaming" on it. And that $300 headset sounds worse than a $150 pair of studio monitors.

What actually matters: input latency on peripherals (mouse sensor, keyboard switch actuation, monitor response time), ergonomics (you'll use this stuff 4+ hours daily), and build quality that survives rage-induced desk impacts. Everything else is aesthetic preference.

This hub collects our tested gear recommendations across every category — plus a PC builder tool that optimizes performance-per-dollar at any budget. Whether you're building a $700 1080p rig or a $3,000 4K workstation, the research is done. You just pick your budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

Wireless or wired mouse for competitive gaming?

Wireless. In 2026, top wireless mice (Razer Viper V3, Logitech Superlight 2) have latency equal to or better than wired mice. The "wired is faster" myth died years ago. Wireless also eliminates cable drag, which actually improves consistency.

Is 4K gaming worth it in 2026?

Only if you have a GPU that can push it (RTX 4080+ or equivalent). At 4K, you need 2-3x the GPU power for the same frame rates as 1440p, and the visual difference on a 27" monitor is subtle. 1440p at 165Hz+ is the sweet spot for most gamers — high resolution, high frame rate, affordable hardware.

Do I need a "gaming" chair?

No. Most gaming chairs are worse than a $300 ergonomic office chair. Racing-style bucket seats look cool but provide terrible lumbar support for long sessions. Get an ergonomic office chair (Herman Miller Aeron, Steelcase Leap, or budget alternatives like the HON Ignition 2.0). Your back will thank you in 10 years.
