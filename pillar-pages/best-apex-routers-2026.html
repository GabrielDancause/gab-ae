How to Choose Your Apex Router

Wi-Fi 7 vs Wi-Fi 6E — Is It Worth the Upgrade?

Yes, but only if you have devices that support it. Wi-Fi 7 introduces Multi-Link Operation (MLO), which lets devices connect across multiple frequency bands simultaneously for lower latency and higher throughput. It also doubles channel width to 320 MHz on the 6 GHz band. If your latest phone, laptop, or VR headset supports Wi-Fi 7, the upgrade delivers real-world speed improvements of 30-50% over Wi-Fi 6E.

Standalone Router vs. Mesh System

If your home is under 2,000 sq ft and your router can sit centrally, a standalone router will outperform any mesh system at the same price. Mesh becomes necessary at 2,500+ sq ft, multi-story homes, or homes with materials that block signals (concrete walls, metal framing). Don't buy mesh because it sounds better — buy it because your floor plan demands it.

The Subscription Problem

NETGEAR and TP-Link both lock security and parental control features behind annual subscriptions ($50-100/year). ASUS includes everything free. Over a 5-year router lifecycle, that's $250-500 in hidden costs on top of the purchase price. Factor subscriptions into your total cost of ownership when comparing.

Do You Actually Need 10 GbE Ports?

Only if your ISP delivers multi-gigabit service (2.5 Gbps+) or you transfer large files between devices on your LAN (NAS, media servers, backups). For 95% of households on 1 Gbps plans, 2.5 GbE is more than sufficient. Don't pay extra for 10 GbE ports you won't use.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a router an "apex" router?

An apex router sits at the top of a manufacturer's lineup — flagship silicon, maximum throughput, multi-gig wired ports, and the full feature set. It's the best a brand offers, not the best value. Think of it as the difference between a Toyota Camry (excellent car) and a Lexus LC 500 (apex product).

Is Wi-Fi 7 backwards compatible with older devices?

Yes, completely. Wi-Fi 7 routers support Wi-Fi 6E, Wi-Fi 6, Wi-Fi 5, and even older standards. Your old laptop will still connect fine — it just won't benefit from the new Wi-Fi 7 features like MLO or 320 MHz channels.

How long do flagship routers typically receive firmware updates?

ASUS typically supports flagship routers for 4-5 years. NETGEAR averages 3-4 years. TP-Link varies by model. Ubiquiti's UniFi line receives updates indefinitely as part of their controller ecosystem. OpenWrt-based routers like the GL.iNet Flint 2 can receive community updates long after manufacturer support ends.

Can I use a Wi-Fi 7 router with a 100 Mbps internet plan?

Absolutely, and you'll still benefit. Better Wi-Fi standards improve local network performance (device-to-device transfers, streaming from a NAS), reduce latency, handle more simultaneous connections, and future-proof your setup. Your internet speed is just one variable — your local network matters too.

Should I buy a mesh system or a router + range extender?

Mesh, every time. Range extenders halve your bandwidth because they receive and retransmit on the same channel. Mesh systems with dedicated backhaul channels maintain full speed between nodes. Range extenders are a band-aid; mesh is a real solution.

What's the best router placement for maximum coverage?

Central location, elevated (shelf or wall-mounted), away from metal objects and microwaves. The #1 mistake is placing the router in a corner of the house near the cable entry point. If you can't relocate your router centrally, that's when mesh becomes necessary.
